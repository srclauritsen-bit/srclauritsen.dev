Growing Up In-Between

Growing up as an expat teenager, I lived in a constant state of in-between — never quite belonging to the culture around me, yet slowly drifting from the one I'd left behind. I learned to read rooms before I spoke, code-switching between languages, customs, and even versions of myself depending on who was watching. Holidays felt different, my humor didn't always translate, and the small references that bonded my peers — childhood shows, slang, shared history — were ones I'd missed or only half-knew.

There was a particular loneliness in being surrounded by people yet feeling like a perpetual outsider, even as I built genuine friendships. And when I returned "home," I found that place had shifted too — I'd changed in ways my passport country couldn't quite recognize, while the place I actually grew up in was never fully mine to claim.

It was a strange kind of grief: missing two places at once, never feeling fully rooted in either.
